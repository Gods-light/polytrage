What make backtest actually returns

Every window is filled at its entry price — the edge at the first qualifying minute, i.e. what a strategy watching in real time could have acted on — never at its peak. Filling at the peak would be lookahead bias: the peak isn't knowable until the window has already closed. The peak is still reported alongside it as a theoretical ceiling, so the gap between the two stays visible instead of quietly assumed away.

Council review · 2026-07-16

A four-voice review, before any edge claim gets trusted

Pursue polytrage as a signal-research / measurement instrument, not a live trading system.— Council consensus, 2026-07-16

  • Cond. 1Freeze fee and slippage as pessimistic constants. Never optimizer-searchable — an optimizer free to lower its own cost assumption always reports a rosier net_profit by cutting costs rather than finding a genuinely better threshold.
  • Cond. 2One event is an anecdote, not evidence. Gate: 20–30 events before any tuning claim is trusted, with a hard go/no-go at that point — see Next milestone.

Fixed in code — verified against source + a real test run

  • (a) Lookahead bias in the backtest fillThe backtester filled each window at its peak edge — unknowable until after the window closed. Now fills at entry: ArbWindow.entry_sum/.entry_edge, computed by backtest/runner.py; peak is retained for reporting only.
  • (b) Slippage was optimizer-tunable (Goodhart)slippage had walked down to $0.0005/leg — the cheapest value ever offered. tuner.py now defines FROZEN_FEE=0.0 / FROZEN_SLIPPAGE=0.002, applied identically to every candidate, outside the search.
  • (c) cli and evolve disagreed on the search gridTwo independent inline grids meant a human run and the nightly job could reach different "best" params from the same data. Both now call one canonical tuner.DEFAULT_GRID().
Fix (c) caught a live regression mid-review: a signature change (slippages→max_gaps) left evolve.py passing slippage values into the gap-merge slot positionally — no exception, just max_gap_s: 0.0005 silently adopted into params.json. Caught, reverted, both call sites fixed. Suite went 59/1 fail → 64/0 fail.

Open risks — accepted, documented, not blocking

  • Walk-forward folds are all cut from one match — they validate temporal drift within a single liquidity regime, not generalization across events, sports, or conditions.
  • Paper backtesting three separately-quoted books can't observe legging risk — the chance of filling one or two legs of a basket and not the third.
  • Best edges cluster right after goals — exactly where co-located bots race to cancel stale quotes. "The backtest may price an opportunity that structurally belongs to someone else." — Critic
  • "Maker-zero-fee may not apply, since capturing a closing window likely needs taker fills." — Skeptic

Full verdict, findings, and verification log: docs/COUNCIL-2026-07-16.md. Two voices (Critic, Skeptic) are quoted by name because the source verdict attributed specific lines to them; the other two participated but aren't individually attributed there either.
